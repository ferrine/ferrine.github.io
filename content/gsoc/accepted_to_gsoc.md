# Hurray!

Yesterday I got an email from [NumFOCUS](https://www.numfocus.org/) with congratulations. 
They said that I had been accepted to GSoC. So summer is going to be full of adventures. I'd like
say many thanks to my mentors, [Taku Yoshioka](https://github.com/taku-y) and [Thomas Wiecki](https://github.com/twiecki), 
for choosing me.

A lot of work is going to be done. I'm very excited with Bayesian stuff and what's the best is 
the feeling that you collaborate with pymc-dev team from all over the word. That you work on
something that not only is needed by yourself but can solve other's people problems. That what you do
can be easily scaled up with GPU and do bayesian inference on really big data. 

We are about the time where risk weighted decisions can be done in Deep Learning. The only thing 
is needed is to reinterpret deterministic model as probabilistic model that predicts not a single 
point but a distribution. There are few libraries that allow to do so ([Edward](https://github.com/blei-lab/edward) 
and [PyMC3](https://github.com/pymc-devs/pymc3)). The main idea behind my proposal is to make VI closer
to the final user, data scientist, that constructs models and solves business problems. That's why
I've started [Gelato](https://github.com/ferrine/gelato) project. It is in not in a release stage yet, but I 
have a strong desire to change it.

To conclude I'm going to have best summer ever!
